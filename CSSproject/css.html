Colorado Symphony Classics 2022-2023

Our 2022/23 Season promises to be an imaginative and spectacular journey through some of the best music ever composed. We’re delighted to present to you a season brimming with extraordinary talent and exciting repertoire that we hope will ignite your imagination. The Colorado Symphony is committed to bringing the best in live, symphonic music to an eclectic and ever-growing audience, and we’re well positioned to embark on an exciting path forward in the years to come.

Colorado Symphony Concert Schedule

Vivaldi's The Four Seasons

Date: OCT 14-16 (FRI-SAT 7:30, SUN 1:00)

Location: Boettcher Concert Hall

Symphonic bliss awaits you as your Colorado Symphony and guest conductor Aram Demirjian present a powerhouse program highlighted by Vivaldi’s programmatic masterpiece, The Four Seasons.

Featured Artists: Aram Demirjian, Paul Huang

Repertoire: JESSIE MONTGOMERY Strum, VIVALDI The Four Seasons, TCHAIKOVSKY Symphony No. 4

Beethoven Symphony No. 3 with Peter Oundjian

Date: NOV 4-6 (FRI-SAT 7:30, SUN 1:00)

Location: Boettcher Concert Hall

History is replete with turning points and with the completion of his revolutionary Third Symphony, Beethoven forever changed the idea of what a symphony could be.

Featured Artists: Peter Oundjian, Sterling Elliott, Colorado Symphony Chorus

Repertoire: VAUGHAN WILLIAMS Toward the Unknown Region, ELGAR Cello Concerto, BEETHOVEN Symphony No. 3 "Eroica"

Tchaikovsky Piano Concerto No. 1 with Olga Kern

Date: DEC 2-4 (FRI-SAT 7:30, SUN 1:00)

Location: Boettcher Concert Hall

Peter Ilyich Tchaikovsky placed himself among the greats of the keyboard with the completion of his First Piano Concerto.

Featured Artists: Christian Reif, Olga Kern

Repertoire: TCHAIKOVSKY Piano Concerto No. 1, SHOSTAKOVICH Symphony No. 10

Ravel’s Daphnis et Chloé

Date: JAN 13-15 (FRI-SAT 7:30, SUN 1:00)

Location: Boettcher Concert Hall

A whirling celebration of pagan love awaits listeners with Ravel's Daphnis et Chloé, which he called a “symphonie chorégraphique” rather than a ballet.

Featured Artists: Kevin John Edusei, Yumi Hwang-Williams, Colorado Symphony Chorus

Repertoire: BOULANGER D'un matin de printemps, SZYMANOWSKI Violin Concerto No. 1, RAVEL Daphnis et Chloé

Beethoven Symphony No. 8

Date: FEB 10-12 (FRI-SAT 7:30, SUN 1:00)

Location: Boettcher Concert Hall

Beethoven’s Eighth — which he referred to as “my little Symphony in F” — is the most cheery, joyful, and experimental of his symphonies.

Featured Artists: Christopher Dragon, Colorado Symphony Horn Section

Repertoire: HAYDN Symphony No. 45 "Farewell", R. SCHUMANN Konzertstück for 4 Horns and Orchestra, BEETHOVEN Symphony No. 8

Beethoven’s Emperor Concerto

Date: FEB 24-26 (FRI-SAT 7:30, SUN 1:00)

Location: Boettcher Concert Hall

One of the most accomplished of all piano works, Beethoven’s “Emperor” Concerto features a striking opening movement and a glorious finale.

Featured Artists: Teddy Abrams, Stewart Goodyear

Repertoire: PERKINSON Worship: A Concert Overture, BEETHOVEN Piano Concerto No. 5 "Emperor", PROKOFIEV Symphony No. 5

Bartók Concerto for Orchestra

Date: MAR 10-12 (FRI-SAT 7:30, SUN 1:00)

Location: Boettcher Concert Hall

Bartók’s Concerto for Orchestra was called a concerto rather than a symphony as each section of instruments is treated in a soloistic and virtuosic way.

Featured Artists: Alexander Shelley, Pablo Sainz Villegas

Repertoire: ANNA CLYNE This Midnight Hour, RODRIGO Concierto de Aranjuez, BARTÓK Concerto for Orchestra

Brahms Requiem with Peter Oundjian

Date: MAR 24-26 (FRI-SAT 7:30, SUN 1:00)

Location: Boettcher Concert Hall

Brahms’ A German Requiem is intended as comfort and solace for those left behind as they mourn the death of others.

Featured Artists: Peter Oundjian, Colorado Symphony Chorus, Karina Gauvin, Joshua Hopkins

Repertoire: WEBER Oberon: Overture, RAVEL Shéhérazade, BRAHMS Ein deutsches Requiem

Beethoven Violin Concerto

Date: APR 21-23 (FRI-SAT 7:30, SUN 1:00)

Location: Boettcher Concert Hall

Join your Colorado Symphony and Principal Conductor Peter Oundjian in welcoming acclaimed soloist James Ehnes for a reinvigoration of Beethoven’s majestic Violin Concerto.

Featured Artists: Peter Oundjian, James Ehnes

Repertoire: BEETHOVEN Violin Concerto, BRUCKNER Symphony No. 7
